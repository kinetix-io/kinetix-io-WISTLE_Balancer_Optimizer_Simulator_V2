WISTLE: Work Instruction & Standard Time = Line Efficiency

Comprehensive Enterprise Manufacturing Optimization Engine | Version 17.3 (Responsive)

1. Introduction & Core Objective

WISTLE is an advanced, algorithm-driven enterprise simulation platform custom-engineered for LG manufacturing lines (AC, RF, WM, TV). The platform’s core objective is to allow Line Optimization Managers to ingest raw cycle times (TAKTDATA) and GERP master standards, dynamically visualize line bottlenecks, and simulate the exact impact of micro-task routing and manpower reallocation without disrupting live shop-floor operations.

2. Dashboard Definitions & Analytics Logic

The Dashboard displays macro-level metrics. The definitions for the primary tracking KPIs are:

  • Tasks: The total number of distinct micro-tasks identified across the ingested matrix layout.
  • Stages: The total number of physical stations/workbenches recognized on the line.
  • Models: The distinct product variations (SKUs) actively calculated in the mixed-model layout.
  • Total MP: The average allocated manpower footprint currently assigned across the active line setup (rounded to the nearest whole operator).
  • Total Work Content: The mathematical average of total cycle time required to build one complete unit across the active models.
  • Overload Stages: The count of stages whose actual operating TAKT currently breaches the user-defined Standard Takt Benchmark.
  • Bottleneck (Max TAKT): The absolute longest stage duration on the line. This single peak physically restricts the maximum output speed of the entire line.
  • Yield Rate (Est.): Projected output completion quality (baseline fixed at 100% for calculation bounds).
  • UPH (Units Per Hour): Total output capability per hour based strictly on the Bottleneck Max TAKT (Formula: 60 / Max Takt).
  • UPPH (Units Per Person Per Hour): Evaluating labor efficiency by dividing the line output by the total operator footprint (Formula: UPH / Total MP).

3. Optimizer Module & Algorithm Operations

The Optimizer is the interactive simulation matrix where the algorithm and manual overrides interact to resolve bottlenecks.

  • Auto-Optimize Algorithm (Cascade Fallback): A heuristic engine that evaluates the line and sorts all breaching bottlenecks from worst to least. It attempts to migrate tasks from the worst bottleneck to adjacent valid stations. If a bottleneck is constrained and completely stuck, the engine automatically falls back to the next worst bottleneck in the list to ensure optimization continues relentlessly.
  • Strict Manpower Constraint: The Auto-Optimizer is mathematically restricted to routing micro-tasks only. It will never auto-add or reduce manpower to clear a bottleneck. Headcount capacity must be changed manually by the user, ensuring the simulation faithfully reflects real-world floor limitations and does not artificially inflate efficiency by quietly adding labor.

4. Reading the Audit Report: Speed vs. Efficiency (The 0.00% Scenario)

The Exhaustive Iteration Ledger tracks every action taken by the algorithm or manual override. The Iteration Number acts as an immutable session ledger that records all configuration changes since login (e.g., toggling constraints, updating models, or running auto-optimize will all increment the run tracker).

The "0.00% Efficiency Shift" Scenario:

During an Auto-Optimize run, you may notice that tasks are heavily shuffled between stations, but the Global SIM Efficiency and LBE Diff show a flat `0.00%` change. This is mathematically correct.

  • The Mechanics: Global Efficiency relies entirely on lowering the Max Bottleneck. If the algorithm encounters a scenario where the primary bottleneck is physically stuck (due to ± constraints or fully packed adjacent stations), it cannot lower the maximum peak time.
  • The Engine's Value: Instead of failing, the algorithm cascades to attack Secondary Bottlenecks. For example, if the primary peak is 0.3875 (stuck), but Stage 15 is sitting at 0.3700 (still breaching the Target Takt), the engine will successfully migrate tasks out of Stage 15 to relieve the operator.
  • The Result: The global output speed does not mathematically increase, but the engine successfully eliminated an overloaded "Red Zone" station, drastically reducing localized defect risks and relieving operator stress.

5. Mathematical Core Formulations

WISTLE isolates and exposes efficiency tracking at three specific levels of granular operation:

A) Global Line Efficiency (Macro Dashboard Scale)

Efficiency (%) = [ Total Mixed-Model Work Content (ΣWC) ] / [ Total Line Manpower (ΣMP) × Max Baseline Line Takt (T_max) ] × 100

B) Model Capability Ledger (Micro Baseline Scale)

Model Ledger Efficiency (%) = [ Isolated GERT Master Standard Time (ST) ] / [ Model Headcount Footprint × Actual Operational Takt Time ] × 100

C) Optimizer Simulation Efficiency (Active Sandbox Scale)

Simulated Efficiency (%) = [ Σ Work Content of Checked Models ] / [ Σ Headcount of Checked Models × Max Takt within Filter Selection ] × 100
